Carbon Offset Explained: Is It Worth It?

Updated April 2026 · 8 min read · No jargon, just honest answers

Every airline, every delivery service, every "green" brand wants you to offset your carbon. But what actually is a carbon offset? Does it work? And is it just a way for polluters to feel better?

What Is a Carbon Offset?

A carbon offset is a certificate that represents 1 tonne of CO₂ removed or prevented from entering the atmosphere. You buy offsets to compensate for emissions you can't avoid — like a necessary flight or your home heating.

Examples of offset projects:

  • 🌳 Reforestation — Planting trees that absorb CO₂ as they grow
  • 💨 Methane capture — Collecting methane from landfills before it enters the atmosphere
  • ⚡ Renewable energy — Funding wind/solar projects that displace coal
  • 🏭 Direct air capture — Machines that pull CO₂ directly from the air
  • 🍳 Clean cookstoves — Replacing wood/charcoal stoves in developing countries

The Good: What Quality Offsets Do Right

  • Additionality — The project wouldn't have happened without offset funding
  • Permanence — The carbon stays sequestered for decades or centuries
  • Verification — Third-party auditors confirm real CO₂ reduction
  • No double counting — The same tonne isn't sold twice

The Bad: Why Cheap Offsets Are Controversial

  • Many cheap forestry credits protect forests that were never at risk
  • Some projects leak emissions — deforestation just moves to another area
  • Trees can burn down, releasing stored carbon back
  • A Guardian investigation (2023) found 90% of Verra's rainforest credits didn't represent real carbon reductions

The Golden Rule: Reduce First, Offset Second

The right approach:

  1. Measure — Calculate your carbon footprint
  2. Reduce — Cut what you can (diet, transport, energy) — see our 20 tips
  3. Offset — Only the emissions you truly can't eliminate

Think of it like recycling: reducing waste is better than recycling, but recycling what you can't reduce is still better than landfilling everything.
