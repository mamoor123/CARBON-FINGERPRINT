Updated April 2026 · 9 min read · Data: Google, MIT, IEA, Hannah Ritchie

Every time you ask ChatGPT a question, somewhere a server lights up and electricity flows. Headlines say AI is destroying the planet. But how bad is it really?

Let's cut through the noise with actual numbers.

The Real Picture: Training vs Inference

AI emissions come from two phases:

Training (one-time cost)

Training a large language model like GPT-4 produces roughly 500–1,500 tonnes of CO₂. That sounds enormous — it's equivalent to the lifetime emissions of 100–300 cars.

But this cost is spread across billions of queries over the model's entire lifetime. Per query, it becomes negligible — fractions of a gram.

Inference (ongoing cost)

This is what happens every time you ask a question. Google estimates its Gemini model uses about 0.24 Wh per query — less than running a microwave for a fraction of a second. The water cost: about 5 drops per query.

Right now, AI is a tiny fraction of global emissions. But it's growing fast — the IEA estimates AI could consume 10x more electricity by 2030 if current trends continue.

Should You Feel Guilty About Using AI?

Short answer: No.

Using ChatGPT for 100 queries produces roughly the same CO₂ as boiling a kettle once. The real concern isn't individual usage — it's scale. When millions of people use AI daily, and when AI is integrated into every search, email, and app, the aggregate energy demand could become significant.

The bigger picture:

  • Big tech companies are racing to power data centers with renewable energy
  • Google claims to match 100% of electricity use with renewables (annual basis)
  • Microsoft is investing in nuclear power for AI data centers
  • Chip efficiency is improving rapidly — newer AI chips are 10–100x more efficient than 2020 models

💡 Context matters: Your personal carbon footprint (4.7 tonnes/year global average) dwarfs any AI usage. One fewer car trip saves more CO₂ than a year of daily ChatGPT queries.

What You Can Actually Do

  1. Don't worry about individual AI queries — they're negligible
  2. Do worry about your transport, diet, and energy — these are 80%+ of your footprint
  3. Support companies investing in clean energy for data centers
  4. Calculate your real footprint — AI queries are a rounding error compared to your daily commute
