The Battery Question

Yes, EV batteries are carbon-intensive to produce. A typical 75 kWh battery produces 4–8 tonnes of CO₂ during manufacturing, depending on where it's made and the energy mix used.

But here's the key: that upfront cost is paid back quickly:

  • On an average grid: EV breaks even at ~20,000 km (about 1.5 years)
  • On a clean grid (France, Norway): EV breaks even at ~10,000 km (less than 1 year)
  • On a coal-heavy grid (Poland): EV breaks even at ~40,000 km (about 3 years)

After break-even, every kilometer driven is pure climate benefit.
