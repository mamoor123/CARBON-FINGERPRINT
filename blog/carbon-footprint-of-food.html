Carbon Footprint of Food: From Steak to Salad

Updated April 2026 · 7 min read · Data: Poore & Nemecek (2018, Science), FAO, IPCC AR6

Food accounts for 26% of global greenhouse gas emissions. But not all food is created equal — the difference between the most and least climate-friendly protein sources is over 50x.

The most comprehensive study on food emissions ever conducted — Poore & Nemecek (2018) — analyzed 38,700 farms across 119 countries. Here's what they found.

Why Is Beef So Bad?

Beef tops the chart for three reasons:

  1. Methane — Cows burp methane (CH₄), which is 80x more potent than CO₂ over 20 years.
  2. Land use — Beef requires 20x more land than beans per gram of protein. Much of this comes from deforestation.
  3. Feed conversion — Cows convert only 3% of calories from feed into edible calories. Chickens convert 13%, fish 16%.

The Biggest Food Waste Problem

30% of all food produced is wasted (FAO). That's 1.3 billion tonnes per year, producing ~3.3 billion tonnes of CO₂ — more than the entire aviation industry. Reducing food waste is one of the easiest climate wins.

💡 Quick win: Switching from a high-meat to a low-meat diet saves ~1.3 tonnes/year — more than switching to an electric car in many countries.

What About "Food Miles"?

Transport accounts for only 6% of food emissions on average. The production method matters far more:

  • Local beef still has 10x the emissions of imported lentils
  • Shipping by boat is incredibly efficient — bananas shipped from Ecuador have lower emissions than local greenhouse-grown tomatoes in winter
  • What you eat matters more than where it comes from
