How to Reduce Your Carbon Footprint: 20 Proven Tips

Updated April 2026 · 12 min read · All CO₂ savings based on IPCC AR6 emission factors

The average person in a developed country produces 8–15 tonnes of CO₂ per year. The 1.5°C target requires 2.3 tonnes. That gap feels huge — but the biggest cuts come from just a handful of changes.

Here are 20 proven actions, ranked by impact. You don't need to do all of them — even 3–4 can cut your footprint in half.

🚗 Transport (Biggest Impact)

Saves 2.0–4.6t/year

1. Go car-free or switch to an EV

The average car emits 4.6 tonnes of CO₂ per year (EPA). Switching to an EV charged on renewable energy drops this to ~0.5t. Going car-free eliminates it entirely. If you can't go car-free, carpooling cuts emissions by 50%+.

Saves 0.8–2.5t/year

2. Fly less (or not at all)

A single transatlantic round trip produces ~1.6 tonnes. A domestic round trip ~0.5t. Take trains where possible — they emit 90% less than flying. If you must fly, choose direct flights and economy class (business class is 3x higher per seat).

Saves 0.5–1.5t/year

3. Bike, walk, or use public transit

Replacing a 20km daily car commute with public transit saves ~1 tonne/year. Biking or walking saves even more — and improves your health.

Saves 0.3–0.8t/year

4. Work from home 2–3 days/week

Remote work eliminates commute emissions. Even 2 days/week reduces your transport footprint by ~40%.

🥗 Food (Second Biggest)

Saves 0.8–1.8t/year

5. Switch to a plant-rich diet

A high-meat diet produces ~3.3t/year. A vegetarian diet ~1.7t. A vegan diet ~1.5t. You don't have to go fully vegan — just cutting beef and lamb by 50% makes a huge difference.

Saves 0.2–0.5t/year

6. Reduce food waste

30% of food is wasted globally. Composting and meal planning can save 0.2–0.5 tonnes/year while also saving money.

Saves 0.1–0.3t/year

7. Buy local and seasonal

Food miles matter less than you think (about 6% of food emissions), but local and seasonal produce avoids energy-intensive greenhouse growing and cold storage.

🏠 Home Energy

Saves 1.0–2.0t/year

8. Switch to renewable electricity

If your grid is coal-heavy, switching to a green energy provider or installing solar can cut 1–2 tonnes/year. In countries with clean grids (France, Sweden), this matters less.

Saves 0.5–1.5t/year

9. Insulate your home

Proper insulation can cut heating bills (and emissions) by 25–40%. Focus on walls, roof, and windows.

Saves 0.3–0.8t/year

10. Use a heat pump instead of gas heating

Heat pumps are 3–5x more efficient than gas boilers. Switching from gas to a heat pump saves 0.5–1.5 tonnes/year depending on your electricity source.

Saves 0.1–0.3t/year

11. Turn down the thermostat 1–2°C

Each degree saves ~8% on heating. Wear a sweater. It's the cheapest energy saving you'll ever make.

Saves 0.1–0.2t/year

12. Switch to LED lighting

LEDs use 75% less energy than incandescent bulbs and last 25x longer. A quick win.

🛒 Consumption

Saves 0.3–1.0t/year

13. Buy less stuff

Every product has hidden emissions: raw materials, manufacturing, shipping. A single cotton t-shirt produces ~7kg CO₂. A smartphone ~70kg. The greenest product is the one you don't buy.

Saves 0.2–0.5t/year

14. Buy secondhand

Used clothes, furniture, electronics — same functionality, fraction of the emissions. Thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, and refurb programs all work.

Saves 0.1–0.3t/year

15. Repair instead of replace

Extend the life of what you already own. Repair cafés, YouTube tutorials, and iFixit guides make this easier than ever.

💬 Advocacy & Systemic Change

Multiplied impact

16. Vote for climate policy

Policy change is the most powerful lever. Carbon taxes, renewable subsidies, and building standards affect millions of people at once. Your vote multiplies your personal impact.

Multiplied impact

17. Talk about it

Research shows that when people learn their friends are taking climate action, they're more likely to do the same. Normalize conversations about carbon footprints.

Multiplied impact

18. Choose climate-conscious employers and banks

Your workplace and bank are powerful. Companies with real climate commitments (not just greenwashing) drive systemic change. Divest from fossil fuels.

🔄 Offset What You Can't Reduce

Varies

19. Offset unavoidable emissions

Flights you can't avoid, essential driving — offset these through verified programs (Gold Standard, Verra). Cost: ~$15–30 per tonne. But always reduce first, offset second.

Long-term

20. Support reforestation and carbon removal

Plant trees, support direct air capture, or fund soil carbon projects. These are long-term investments in drawing down existing CO₂.
