1. Overview

Carbon Fingerprint estimates your annual carbon footprint based on 15 lifestyle questions across 5 categories: Transport, Diet, Energy, Consumption, and Flights. Each question maps to peer-reviewed emission factors.

The calculation is:

Total = Transport + Diet + Energy + Consumption + Flights + Shared (1.5t)

Every emission factor includes an uncertainty range (low/high). Your total footprint is displayed as a single value with a 95% confidence interval derived from propagating individual uncertainties.

7. Shared / Base Emissions

Value: 1.5 tonnes CO₂e/year (range: 1.0 – 2.5)

This represents your share of emissions from government services, public infrastructure, military, healthcare, and education — things you benefit from as a citizen but don't directly control.

Source: Our World in Data, derived from government expenditure emissions data. The 1.5t figure is a per-capita allocation for OECD countries.

Note: This is a simplified allocation. Actual shared emissions vary significantly by country (lower in developing nations, higher in high-defense-spending countries).

8. Uncertainty Ranges

Every emission factor has an uncertainty range representing the 95% confidence interval. These arise from:

  • Regional variation: Grid electricity ranges from 20g CO₂/kWh (Norway) to 900g (Poland)
  • Individual behavior: Driving style, home insulation, cooking habits
  • Methodology: Different LCA boundaries produce different results
  • Temporal variation: Seasonal changes, weather anomalies

Your total uncertainty is calculated by summing the low and high bounds independently across all categories. This is a conservative approach — in reality, uncertainties partially cancel out, so the true range is narrower.

We display: Central estimate (low – high) tonnes CO₂e/year

10. Climate Data Sources

NASA GISS Surface Temperature Analysis (GISTEMP v4)

Global temperature anomalies from 1880–2025, relative to the 1951–1980 baseline. This is real measured data, not estimates.

https://data.giss.nasa.gov/gistemp/

Mauna Loa CO₂ Record

Atmospheric CO₂ concentration measured at Mauna Loa Observatory, Hawaii. Current value: ~421 ppm (2024 average).

https://gml.noaa.gov/ccgg/trends/

City Climate Data

City-level climate indicators (local temperature, sea level rise, extreme weather days) are sourced from IPCC AR6 Working Group II regional chapters and national meteorological services. These are estimates based on regional projections, not direct measurements for each city.

11. Assumptions

⚠️ Key Assumptions
  • Average lifestyle within each category: Each question represents a broad lifestyle pattern. Your actual emissions may differ.
  • OECD-centric shared emissions: The 1.5t base is calibrated for developed countries. Residents of developing countries may have lower shared emissions.
  • No offsetting: We calculate gross emissions, not net. If you purchase carbon offsets, your net footprint is lower.
  • Annual averages: Seasonal variations (winter heating, summer AC) are averaged out.
  • No embodied emissions of housing: Construction emissions of your home are not included.
  • Flight class multiplier is simplified: Actual per-seat emissions depend on aircraft type, load factor, and route.

12. Limitations

🔴 Known Limitations
  • 15 questions can't capture everything: A full lifecycle assessment would require hundreds of data points. This is an estimate, not an audit.
  • No country-specific grid factors: We use 4 electricity categories, but actual grid emissions vary enormously (France: 50g/kWh, Poland: 700g/kWh).
  • No seasonal adjustment: Heating/cooling demand varies by climate and season.
  • Flight emissions ignore radiative forcing: Non-CO₂ effects (contrails, NOx) roughly double aviation's climate impact. We report CO₂ only.
  • Food emissions are averages: Organic vs conventional, local vs imported, seasonal vs year-round — all matter but aren't captured.
  • No supply chain detail: Where your products are manufactured matters, but we can't capture that in a 60-second quiz.

13. Comparison to Other Calculators

Different calculators produce different results because they use different methodologies, emission factors, and system boundaries. Here's how we compare:

CalculatorApproachTypical Difference
EPA (US) Detailed US-specific factors EPA may be 10–20% higher for US residents (US-specific grid)
WWF (UK/EU) Consumption-based, includes imports WWF may be 15–25% higher (includes embedded imports)
UN Carbon Footprint Simplified, global averages Similar range, may differ on transport
Footprint.org Ecological footprint (gha, not tonnes) Different unit — not directly comparable

Why we might differ: We use IPCC AR6 factors (2022), which are more recent than many calculators. We also include shared/government emissions (1.5t) which many calculators omit.
